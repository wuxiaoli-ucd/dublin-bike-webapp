**Interview Questions and Answers (Persona 2, Conor)** 

*Products \- (Understand the current system-as-is)*

**Have you ever used a bike rental service before?**  
Never before my bike broke down.

**Can you briefly describe the process of using Dublin Bikes?**  
When I realised my bike wasn’t working, I thought of alternative ways to get to work. If I were to go by bus, I would have to change once and it would take twice as long as cycling. As I cycle to work every day, I have seen others using Dublin Bikes so I knew they were popular. I searched the app on the app store and it downloaded quickly and I was able to immediately start searching for bike parking. It was a 10 minute walk to the closest one.

**How do you check bike availability?**  
It’s easy to check availability on the app as it tells you how many bikes there are. 

**How do you unlock the bike?**  
I followed the instructions in the app, it took a couple minutes my first time which was frustrating because I was in a rush, but I would be confident it wouldn’t take so long in the future. 

**How do you make payments?**  
I used Apple Pay.

**Do you experience any issues in the current process?**  
The system to log credit is annoying, I would like to just pay for my trip. The bikes are too uniform in size also. Maybe it would be useful to have smaller and larger bikes, which could show on the app. As a short woman, I found it too big even if you can adjust the seat size. 

**Have you ever failed to reserve a bike? Why?**  
I haven’t used the service long enough to experience that.

**Have you ever rented a damaged bike? How easy was it to get a refund or find another bike?**  
Again, this hasn’t happened. I am aware that there’s a support section in the app, but I haven’t had to use it yet.

**Would you value a system where you could reserve a bike in advance?**  
Yes that would be useful so I’m not worried about availability. 

**What information do you need before reserving a bike?**

**What confirmation do you expect after booking?**  
An in-app confirmation would be enough. If I used it more often, it would be annoying to get emails all the time.

**How would you prefer to unlock a rental bike?**  
The current system is a little complicated with both the physical and in-app actions required. For example, it would be easy to make mistakes about which bike you’re unlocking. When I used Bolt electric bikes abroad, you could just scan the QR code on the bike and you could ride straight away. 

**Would you be accepting of marketing after a certain amount of use that would reward loyalty to Dublin Bikes?**

Yes, maybe it would be useful to get a free hour after 10 trips. 

**If you could change one thing about the current bike rental experience, what would it be?**  
I was annoyed that I had to log credit in the app (5 euro) before using a bike even though I knew my trip wouldn’t cost that much. It should be possible to just pay for the journey you undertake. 

**Interview Questions and Answers (Persona 3, Andrew)** 

*Products \- (Understand the current system-as-is)*

**Have you ever used a bike rental service before?**  
Yes, I’ve given a few services a try around Dublin. I prefer to bike after getting off of the train so that I’m not sitting for so long.

**Can you briefly describe the process?**  
I have an account already set up with a couple apps, so it’s fairly easy now. I just pull up the app and see what bikes are available in my area. When I clicked on their icon, I get asked if I want to pay to rent it or not.

**How do you check bike availability?**  
GPS, I think? I’m exactly sure how the apps work, but I would assume it’s something like that. It just pings up on a map what is available in my area.

**How do you unlock the bike?**  
Once you pay for the rental the bike will unlock from the station that it’s parked in. Sometimes it takes a couple minutes, which is frustrating if I’m running late.

**How do you make payments?**  
I like to use Revolut, although one of the apps will only use my debit card.

**Do you experience any issues in the current process?**  
My only real issue is usually availability. Nobody wants to get stuck in traffic or sit on a bus all day, so bike rental is a pretty popular way to commute in the city. And the tourists like to take them all at any time of day.

**Have you ever failed to reserve a bike? Why?**  
Yeah, like I said, sometimes during peak hours there just aren’t enough available. Occasionally there is also an issue with the app accepting payment, although I think that’s more of an internet issue.

**Have you ever rented a damaged bike? How easy was it to get a refund or find another bike?**  
Yes\! It was so frustrating. I was able to get a refund, but it took a couple days of emailing back and forth with customer service, and I wasn’t able to easily get a replacement rental that day. I ended up having to use another app.

**What information do you need before reserving a bike?**  
Most of the apps wanted an email, phone number and payment info. The usual stuff for most app services.

**What confirmation do you expect after booking?**  
An email or text with a receipt. I need it for my budget and taxes, and it’s nice to have in case I have any issues for customer service.

Receipts, yes. Trip summaries? I don’t think so. Maybe it’s something in the settings that I have to turn on?

30 minutes to an hour. The only time I ever really go over 30 is if I need to stop for some food before work.

Sure\! I’d love to get free rides. I use bike services a lot, so a reward program would be grand.

Just the overall speed of rental, I suppose. I want to be able to either reserve a bike ahead of time, or to be able to get one up and running in less than a couple minutes.
